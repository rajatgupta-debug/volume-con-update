Hexagons are the most efficient shape for tiling a surface — they provide the maximum area for the minimum perimeter. This is the Honeycomb Conjecture, first proposed in 36 BC by Marcus Terentius Varro and finally proved by mathematician Thomas Hales in 1999. Bees figured it out millions of years before us.
