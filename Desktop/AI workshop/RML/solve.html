Why Honeycombs are Hexagons 🐝
Step 3 of 6
1 Step 1
Multiple Choice

Bees need to store honey and raise their young in cells. They build these cells from wax, which is expensive to produce — a bee must consume about 8 pounds of honey to produce just 1 pound of wax.

So bees need a shape that:
1. Tiles perfectly with no gaps
2. Uses the least wax (perimeter) for the most storage (area)

Before we compare efficiency, let's first figure out which shapes can even tile a flat surface without leaving gaps.

You've now seen that hexagons tile perfectly AND provide the most area per unit perimeter. This is called the Honeycomb Conjecture — a shape-efficiency problem pondered since ancient Rome, and finally proved mathematically by Thomas Hales in 1999.
