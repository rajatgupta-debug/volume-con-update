# Real Math League — Executive Synthesis

## Thesis

RML is a free, web-based challenge platform on cuemath.com that turns real-world curiosity into math exploration — a zero-CAC acquisition engine that builds brand love at scale and feeds the paid tutoring funnel.

## Why Now

1. **Cuemath Studio proved the concept.** The YouTube channel (GPS, Tiki-Taka, Honeycombs, Fair Elections, Ellipses) demonstrated that real-world math content generates genuine excitement. The problem: the audience was math enthusiasts, not parents buying tutoring. RML takes the same creative DNA and puts it in front of kids, on our own domain, with a conversion path.

2. **PLG complements paid marketing.** Paid channels are producing admissions, but the website currently delivers only ~50 admissions/month against a 2,000-2,500/year target. RML gives cuemath.com a reason for kids to visit, stay, return, and eventually convert — without incremental ad spend.

3. **School distribution = zero-CAC at scale.** Teachers are always looking for engaging math enrichment. A free, high-quality challenge platform is an easy yes for any school. Each school that adopts RML brings an entire cohort of families onto cuemath.com. This is the distribution hack that Playground and Academy alone cannot achieve.

4. **Brand positioning opportunity.** The competitive landscape is remediation (Kumon) or pressure-cooker (RSM). RML plants a flag in "excellence through wonder" — a space nobody else occupies. Every challenge card shared on social media is a brand impression that says: Cuemath makes math thrilling, not threatening.

## Key Decisions Made

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Platform | Desktop-first web on cuemath.com | SEO value, no app-store friction, parents discover on desktop |
| Go-to-market | PLG — start without signup | Eliminate every barrier to first "wow" moment |
| Launch scope | 100 challenges across 10 themes, 7 levels | Enough depth for 6+ months of engagement |
| Content team | Karan's team, AI-assisted creation | Speed + creative quality; AI drafts, humans curate for delight |
| Timeline | 6-8 weeks to launch | Aggressive but scoped; challenges are content, not engineering |
| Progression | Accept → Complete/Give-up, one active challenge at a time | Focus + completion incentive |

## Cardinal Rules

1. **Never intimidating.** Every child who opens a challenge should feel invited, not tested. No timers. No red "wrong" screens. Difficulty comes from depth, not pressure.
2. **Never boring.** If the hook doesn't make a kid lean forward, kill it. Every challenge must start with a genuine "wait, what?" moment.
3. **Never traditional.** No "A train leaves Chicago at 60 mph." If it could appear in a textbook, it doesn't belong here.

## Strategic Fit

Cuemath's free surface ecosystem:

| Surface | Purpose | Audience | Engagement Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Playground** | Free math games | Young kids, casual | Quick sessions, fun-first |
| **Academy** | Free structured courses | Serious learners | Sequential, curriculum-aligned |
| **MathGym** | Daily practice app | Enrolled + free users | Habit, streaks, fitness |
| **RML** | Real-world math explorations | Curious kids (grades 3-10) | Wow-driven, challenge-based, social |

RML is the **"wow" layer** — the surface that creates the emotional connection with math that none of the others deliver. Playground is fun but shallow. Academy is deep but earnest. MathGym is disciplined but routine. RML is where a kid discovers that honeycombs are hexagons because of math, that Spotify's shuffle isn't random, that a penalty kick is a geometry problem — and thinks, "I want more of this."

## Risk Call-Outs

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Content quality at scale.** 100 challenges in 6-8 weeks is ambitious. AI-assisted drafts could feel formulaic. | High | Karan's team curates ruthlessly. Better to launch with 70 great challenges than 100 mediocre ones. Quality bar: would this make a Cuemath Studio video? |
| **School adoption slower than projected.** Teachers are busy; free tools still need onboarding. | Medium | Start with Indian-American community schools and Saturday math circles where Cuemath already has relationships. Build case studies before scaling outreach. |
| **Conversion to tutoring is weak.** Kids love RML but see no reason to pay for 1:1. | Medium | Nudges must feel like a natural next step, not an ad. "You crushed Level 5 geometry — imagine what you could do with a personal math coach." Test messaging from week 1. |
| **Desktop-first limits reach.** Kids increasingly default to phones/tablets. | Medium | Responsive design from day one, even if optimized for desktop. Mobile-native version in Phase 2 based on usage data. |
| **Engagement drops after initial novelty.** Kids complete a few challenges and churn. | Medium | Leaderboards (school, city, grade), streaks, seasonal events, new challenge drops every 2 weeks. Social mechanics are the retention layer. |
| **Brand dilution.** If RML feels like a marketing funnel rather than a gift, trust erodes. | High | The product must be genuinely excellent as a standalone experience. Tutoring nudges are contextual and sparse. If a kid never converts, RML should still make them love math — and love Cuemath's brand. |
