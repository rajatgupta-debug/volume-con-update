# Real Math League — Growth Engine

## PLG Funnel

The core insight: signup is the reward gate, not the entry gate.

1. **Land** — User arrives at `cuemath.com/league` via search, social share, school link, or cuemath.com nav
2. **Browse** — Full challenge library visible immediately. No signup required. Filter by theme, grade level, popularity
3. **Accept** — Tap any challenge to start. Still no signup. Zero friction to first value
4. **Complete** — Finish the challenge. See score, key insight, celebration. Experience the full "aha" moment
5. **Gate** — To save progress, earn XP, appear on the leaderboard, or share a branded card, sign up (email or Google). The user already knows the product is worth it

This is the inverse of most edtech funnels. We let the product sell itself before asking for anything.

**Signup triggers (in order of urgency):**
- Save your score and progress
- Get your position on the leaderboard
- Earn XP toward your next level
- Unlock your shareable completion card
- Track your MathFit profile over time

## Viral Loops

### 1. Share Card

A beautiful, branded completion card generated after every challenge. Designed for WhatsApp and Instagram Stories.

**Card contains:** Challenge illustration, challenge name, user's score, a teaser hook, Cuemath branding, QR code / link to that challenge.

**Example copy:** *"I just discovered why honeycombs are hexagons. Can you?"*

The card is the ad. Every completed challenge produces a micro-campaign shared by the user into their network. Parents share to parent groups. Kids share to class groups. Teachers share to faculty channels.

**Design requirements:**
- Aspect ratio optimized for WhatsApp forward and Instagram Story
- Bold, vibrant, unmistakably Cuemath
- Challenge-specific illustration (not generic)
- One-tap share to WhatsApp, Instagram, copy link

### 2. School Leaderboard

Schools compete against each other on aggregate participation and scores. This creates network effects at the institutional level.

- A teacher shares RML with their class → 30 students join → school appears on leaderboard
- Neighboring schools see the leaderboard → competitive pressure → more schools join
- Schools share their ranking on social media, assemblies, newsletters

**Leaderboard mechanics:**
- Ranked by total XP earned (rewards participation + performance)
- Weekly resets to keep competition fresh, all-time board for prestige
- Top 10 schools highlighted with badges
- "Find your school" search to drive first engagement

### 3. Challenge-a-Friend

Direct challenge link: "I scored 850 on 'The Pizza Problem.' Beat me?" Opens that specific challenge with the friend's score as the benchmark.

- One-tap generation from completion screen
- Deep link directly to the challenge
- Shows challenger's score as the target to beat
- Creates 1:1 competitive loops within friend groups

## School Distribution Strategy

RML is positioned as a free tool for math teachers globally. Not a product to sell — a resource to give.

### Value proposition to teachers
*"Show your students a different side of math."*

- Free, no strings attached
- Curriculum-adjacent real-world problems
- Class-level progress visibility via teacher dashboard
- Makes the teacher look innovative

### Teacher dashboard (MVP)
- Add students by class/section
- See completion rates per challenge
- View class leaderboard
- Assign specific challenges to class (share via link)
- No grading burden — RML handles scoring

### Outreach channels
1. **Cuemath's existing school network** — Instant distribution to schools already familiar with Cuemath
2. **Cold email to math teachers** — Targeted outreach with a simple pitch and one-click class setup
3. **Teacher communities** — Reddit (r/matheducation, r/teachers), Facebook groups, teacher forums
4. **Social media** — Short-form content showing real challenges, student reactions
5. **Education conferences and events** — Demo booth / sponsor with QR codes to challenges
6. **Cuemath tutors as ambassadors** — Each tutor shares RML with their students' schools

### School onboarding flow
1. Teacher clicks "Set up your class" from any RML page
2. Creates account (if new), enters school name, grade, section
3. Gets a unique class join link
4. Shares link with students — they join with one click
5. Teacher sees real-time class dashboard

## Conversion to Tutoring

RML users are warm leads who already associate Cuemath with engaging math. Conversion to tutoring ($2,400 LTV) is the monetization layer, but it must never feel like a bait-and-switch.

**Cardinal rule:** The nudge must feel like a natural next step, not a sales pitch. RML is genuinely free forever.

### Nudge Surface 1: Post-Challenge (Contextual)

Appears on the completion screen after a challenge. Soft, relevant, non-intrusive.

> *"Loved exploring why bridges use triangles? Go deeper with a 1:1 Cuemath tutor who makes every concept click like this."*

- Only shown after 2+ completed challenges (earned attention)
- Copy is specific to the challenge theme, not generic
- Single CTA: "Explore tutoring" → links to trial booking
- Dismissible, never repeated for same user in same session

### Nudge Surface 2: Profile / Progress Page (Personalized)

On the signed-in user's profile, a persistent but tasteful section.

> *"Your MathFit score suggests you'd thrive with structured tutoring. Students like you improve 2x faster with a Cuemath tutor."*

- Based on user's actual performance data
- Shows specific areas where tutoring could help
- Social proof: "X students who started on RML now learn with Cuemath tutors"
- CTA: "Book a free trial class"

### Nudge Surface 3: Parent Email (Weekly Digest)

Weekly email to the parent (signup captures parent email for minors).

**Email contains:**
- Challenges completed this week
- XP earned, level progress
- One highlight insight ("This week, your child discovered why GPS needs 4 satellites")
- Soft tutoring CTA at bottom: "Want to take their math journey further?"

- Arrives Sunday evening (pre-week planning mindset)
- First email after 1 week of activity (not immediately)
- Unsubscribable, CAN-SPAM compliant
- A/B test with vs. without tutoring CTA to measure impact on RML retention

## Measurement Framework

### Primary Metrics (North Stars)
- **MAU** — Monthly active users on RML
- **Challenges completed / user / month** — Engagement depth
- **Tutoring trial bookings from RML** — Conversion

### Secondary Metrics
- **Signup rate** — % of anonymous users who sign up
- **Share rate** — % of completions that generate a share
- **School activation rate** — % of onboarded schools with 10+ active students
- **Return rate** — % of users who return within 7 days
- **Challenge completion rate** — % of started challenges that are finished

### Tracking Plan
- Anonymous session tracking (cookie-based) for pre-signup behavior
- Event tracking: page_view, challenge_start, step_complete, challenge_complete, signup, share, nudge_impression, nudge_click, trial_booked
- UTM parameters on all shared links for attribution
- School-level cohort analysis

### A/B Testing Plan
- **Signup gate timing:** After 1st challenge vs. 2nd vs. 3rd
- **Nudge copy and placement:** Post-challenge CTA variations
- **Share card design:** Multiple templates tested for share rate
- **Email cadence:** Weekly vs. bi-weekly parent digest
- **Homepage layout:** Challenge-first vs. story-first

## AEO / SEO Value

Every challenge gets a dedicated, indexable page at `cuemath.com/league/{challenge-slug}`.

**SEO benefits:**
- Hundreds of high-quality, unique content pages on cuemath.com
- Long-tail keyword targeting: "why are honeycombs hexagons math," "real world math problems for kids," "math exploration grade 5"
- Each page has rich structured data (educational content schema)
- Internal linking between challenges, themes, and grade levels strengthens site architecture

**AEO benefits (AI Engine Optimization):**
- Challenge pages are structured as questions + detailed explorations — ideal for AI citation
- Cuemath becomes a source for "real-world math examples" queries in AI assistants
- Rich, original content that AI models prefer to cite over generic educational sites

**Domain authority impact:**
- 100+ unique pages at launch, growing to 500+ in year 1
- High engagement metrics (time on page, low bounce) signal quality to search engines
- Backlink potential: teachers and education blogs linking to specific challenges as resources
